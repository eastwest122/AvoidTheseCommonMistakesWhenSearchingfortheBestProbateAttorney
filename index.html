What Is Probate & Why Proper Legal Help Matters

Probate is the legal process by which a deceased person’s estate is administered — validating the will, paying creditors, taxes, and distributing assets to heirs or beneficiaries.

Estate planning refers to preparing documents (wills, trusts, power of attorney, etc.) in advance to decide how assets will be distributed and who will manage them.

When probate is handled without expert guidance, assets can be overlooked, delays happen, and costs skyrocket. That’s why it’s essential to hire the best probate attorney who also understands estate planning.

Common Mistakes People Make When Choosing a Probate Attorney

Here are 10 common mistakes people make when trying to find the best probate attorney. Avoiding them can save time, money, and emotional energy.

Mistake #1: Hiring Someone Without Probate Experience

Many people choose a general attorney instead of someone who specializes in probate. Without in-depth knowledge of estate planning and local probate laws, mistakes can happen easily.

Always verify that your attorney is experienced in handling probate cases. Ask how many they’ve done and what percentage of their practice is dedicated to estate planning and probate.

Mistake #2: Focusing Too Much on Price Over Quality

It’s tempting to choose the cheapest option when hiring a probate attorney. But focusing only on price can backfire if it means hiring someone who lacks experience or offers minimal support.

The best probate attorney may not be the cheapest — but they will save you time, money, and stress in the long run. Always weigh value and results over price alone.

Mistake #3: Not Checking Credentials and Reviews

Many people hire an attorney without verifying their qualifications or reading client feedback. This can lead to hiring someone unlicensed, inexperienced, or poorly reviewed.

Always verify credentials with your state bar, and read reviews online. The best estate planning attorney will have a proven track record in probate law and satisfied clients.

Mistake #4: Misunderstanding the Role of Communication

Clear and timely communication is essential in probate. If your attorney isn’t responsive or doesn't explain things well, you could be left confused or unprepared for key steps in the process.

Choose a probate attorney who communicates clearly, sets expectations, and keeps you informed. Communication is just as important as legal skill.

Mistake #5: Overlooking Estate Planning & Probate Integration

Some attorneys only handle probate and don’t offer broader estate planning services. This can be a problem if your estate documents need updates or are missing key components.

The best estate planning lawyer integrates both probate and long-term planning. They ensure that your documents are aligned and up to date, reducing complications later.

Mistake #6: Delaying the Process or Procrastinating

Waiting too long to start probate or consult an attorney can cause delays, missed deadlines, and legal issues. Time is especially important when notifying creditors or managing estate taxes.

Don’t wait. Hiring the best probate attorney early ensures everything is done on time and correctly — saving you from unnecessary stress and expense.

Mistake #7: Failing to Understand Fee Structures

Many people don't ask how fees work. Is it hourly, flat fee, or a percentage of the estate? Are there additional filing or court costs?

A trustworthy estate planning attorney will explain their fees clearly up front. Make sure you get a written agreement and understand all potential charges.

Mistake #8: Overlooking State or Local Probate Laws

Probate laws vary from state to state — even from county to county. Using a lawyer unfamiliar with your local laws can lead to rejected filings or costly delays.

Always choose a probate lawyer licensed and experienced in your state. The best probate lawyer knows how local probate courts work and can navigate them efficiently.

Mistake #9: Ignoring Digital & Non-Traditional Assets

Many families forget to include digital assets like crypto wallets, domain names, or social media accounts in their estate plan. These assets can easily be lost or mismanaged.

A qualified estate planning lawyer will help you document and plan for digital assets, ensuring nothing is forgotten or left inaccessible to heirs.

Mistake #10: Not Preparing Alternatives to Probate

Some people assume probate is unavoidable, but in many cases, it’s possible to reduce or avoid it through smart estate planning tools like living trusts, joint ownership, or payable-on-death accounts.

The best estate planning attorney will explore options to simplify or bypass probate — saving your family time, money, and legal hassle.

7 Key Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Probate Attorney

Before hiring someone to handle your family’s estate, be sure to ask these essential questions. They can help you avoid common mistakes and choose the best probate attorney for your needs.

  1. What percentage of your practice is focused on probate and estate planning? – You want someone who regularly works in this field, not someone who dabbles in it.
  2. How familiar are you with the local Surrogate’s Court? – Local experience can streamline your case and prevent delays.
  3. Do you also handle estate planning, or just probate? – A lawyer who integrates both will offer long-term value and strategic advice.
  4. What is your fee structure? – Ask whether it’s hourly, flat fee, or estate percentage — and what that includes.
  5. Will you be the one handling my case personally? – Avoid surprises by knowing if your case is being passed to someone else.
  6. How will we communicate? – Confirm if you’ll receive regular updates, phone calls, or emails, and how quickly they respond.
  7. Can you provide testimonials or client references? – A confident, experienced probate attorney should have positive feedback from past clients.
